Why Simple Sites Have the Best UX

The paradox of design: adding more features usually destroys the User Experience.

Muhammad Ramzan

Founder, Ramzan Digital • Business Architect

Oct 30, 2024
4 min read

Have you ever visited a website and been immediately hit by a loading spinner, followed by a cookie popup, an email newsletter popup, and a background video that makes your phone freeze?

That is what happens when businesses focus on "features" instead of "friction."

Cognitive Overload

Human beings have a very limited amount of short-term memory. In web design, this translates to "Cognitive Load." If a user has to think about where to click, you have already lost them.

Simple websites win because they respect the user's cognitive limits. A clean white background, highly legible black text (like the Inter font), and a single, bright blue button telling them exactly what to do.

"Design is not just what it looks like. Design is how it works." – Steve Jobs

Speed is a Feature

Simplicity isn't just visual; it's architectural. A simple website has fewer DOM elements, zero heavy JavaScript libraries, and no bloatware. This means it loads instantly.

When a site loads instantly, the user feels a sense of control. The UI responds immediately to their taps. This builds subconscious trust. When a user trusts your interface, they are exponentially more likely to trust your business and fill out your lead form.
